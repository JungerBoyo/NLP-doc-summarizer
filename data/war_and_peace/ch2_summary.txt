Anna Pávlovna hosts a reception for Petersburg’s elite, including Hélène, Princess Bolkónskaya, and Pierre, an awkward newcomer. Guests are introduced to Anna’s aunt in a formal but indifferent ritual. The charming princess captivates everyone, while Pierre’s unconventional behavior and critique of Abbé Morio’s ideas disrupt the decorum. Anna expertly maintains the flow of conversation, despite Pierre’s awkwardness, showcasing the intricate social dynamics of Russian aristocracy.
