Ishmael, the narrator, sets off for Cape Horn and the Pacific, passing through New Bedford, where he faces disappointment after missing the Nantucket packet. He reflects on Nantucket's historical significance in whaling and his desire to sail from there. With time to kill before his journey, Ishmael struggles to find affordable lodging and eventually stumbles upon "The Spouter-Inn." The inn's dilapidated appearance and eerie atmosphere evoke thoughts of cold and discomfort, but Ishmael decides it's the best option for a cheap stay. As he considers his surroundings, he contemplates the harshness of winter and the life of a whaler.
